In an open container, add the water, vinegar, and dish soap. Then drop in the baking soda and watch your volcano explode!


Why does this happen?

The baking soda and vinegar mixture causes an acid-base reaction. Given that baking soda is a basic bicarbonate (NaHCO3) and vinegar is an acetic acid (HCH3COO), the reaction causes the formation of carbon dioxide. During the reaction, the vinegar and baking soda are exchanging atoms, more specifically protons. When the base (b,aking soda) receives the proton from the acid (vinegar), it turns into water and carbon dioxide and releases a gas. This is what you see when it bubbles out of the top!
